CIBM Bank Announces New Branch in Wheaton, Illinois
March 12, 2015

CHAMPAIGN, IL--(Marketwired - Mar 11, 2015) - CIBM Bank is proud to announce its expansion into the Chicagoland market with the opening of a new branch location at 330 S. Naperville Road, Suite 100, Wheaton, Illinois. The full-service branch will operate under the name of "Avenue Bank, a branch of CIBM Bank" and is slated to open in May 2015.

"We are excited to build upon the strong relationships our Avenue Mortgage division has developed within the Chicagoland market, and look forward to serving the Wheaton community with our full array of personal banking, business banking and treasury management services," said Mr. John Hickey, CIBM Bank's President and CEO.

CIBM Bank is currently seeking staff for the new branch, and will be hosting Instant Interview Days at its Avenue Mortgage division office located at 1811 Centre Point Drive, Suite 123, Naperville, Illinois on Thursday, March 12th (10am to 3pm) and Friday, March 13th (9am to 2pm). No appointment is necessary. Interested applicants are encouraged to bring their resumes and questions with them to the Instant Interview Days and to visit www.cibmcareers.com to learn more about employment with CIBM Bank.

CIBM Bank is a wholly owned subsidiary of CIB Marine Bancshares, Inc. that operates as "Marine Bank" in its Indiana and Wisconsin markets, "Central Illinois Bank" in its central Illinois market, and "Avenue Bank" in its Chicagoland market. Avenue Mortgage is a division of the bank and located in Naperville, IL. Equal housing lender. Member FDIC.

HSA's on the Rise
August 1, 1014

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) were established in 2003 so that individuals covered by high-deductible health plans could receive tax-preferred treatment of money saved for medical expenses. Generally, an adult who is covered by a high-deductible health plan (and has no other first-dollar coverage) may establish an HSA. Health Savings Accounts differ from Flex Spending Accounts (FSAs) and Health Reimbursement Accounts (HRAs). They are owned by the individual and funds roll over year to year if not spent. HSAs may currently be used to pay for qualified medical expenses.

According to the Society for Human Resource Management, the number of businesses offering HSAs has risen dramatically recently. For more information visit: http://www.shrm.org/hrdisciplines/benefits/articles/pages/hsas-outpacing-hras.aspx

As a business owner, a high deductible health plan may be right for your company. Health Savings Accounts can be a great addition to your company’s benefit package as they can be a good way for you and your employees to supplement that higher deductible.

As an individual participating in a high deductible health plan, whether you are planning ahead to pay for unexpected medical expenses or strategizing to offset your high deductible, a HSA could help you save money.
